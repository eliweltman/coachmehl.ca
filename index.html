About Roger

A coach who has spent 28 years helping people unlock themselves.

Roger Mehl has been a coach in different forms for 28 years. An award-winning graduate of the prestigious London Studio Centre in London, UK — a college for theatre arts professionals — he began by coaching aspiring actors and singers, prized for helping students build their own internal awareness and refine technique from the inside out. A former competitive swimmer, he carried the same approach into a private swimming school for preteens.

In 2016 he developed a workshop called The Art of Listening — a total reframe on how to listen at the deepest levels, hold space, and be fully present with another person. In 2017 he began facilitating year-long leadership development workshops for CEOs, business owners, and management teams, where accountability was the central theme. He built a “laboratory” approach to follow-through: when a participant stalled, he searched for the inner and outer blockages standing between them and the outcome they wanted.

Sponsoring dozens of people through twelve-step recovery sharpened his eye for blind spots, unconscious decisions, and self-sabotage.

Roger sees himself as a bridger — a term coined by Lynn Weiss, PhD, in Embracing A.D.D.: A Healing Perspective. A bridger shares the wiring of an ADHDer while adapting reasonably well to a more linear thinking style. He doesn’t coach ADHD from a textbook — he coaches it from the inside.

In 2025 Roger began freelancing full-time, steadily acquiring ADHD professionals and diving deep into the study and practice of ADHD coaching. He lives in Toronto with his wife and kids.

Chapter Three · Made With Heroes and Dragonslayers In Mind

ADHDers often carry a staunch resistance to accepting recent, well-researched findings — namely, that they are brilliant, essential to the workplace, and strategically invaluable to their teams. In these pages, you’ll learn to dislodge the stake of that resistance — however big or small — and discover the full bloom of your ADHD talents.

You may already have been through a slew of resources that didn’t help — books, podcasts, influencers, workshops, that ten-page, over-hyphenated habit-building strategy you had ChatGPT generate. Before we go any further: take a minute to appreciate everything you’ve done to succeed in a society that doesn’t yet appreciate your gifts.

You are not lazy. You are extremely motivated. If you achieved averagely, you’re a hero. If you’ve gone further than that, you’re a dragonslayer.
