About Yermi

A coach who's spent 28 years helping people unlock themselves.

Yermi Mel began by coaching performers — trained at the prestigious London Studio Centre — where he discovered a gift for helping people build deep internal awareness and refine their craft from the inside out.

He went on to lead year-long leadership programs for CEOs, founders, and management teams, where accountability became his signature. He built a “laboratory” approach to follow-through: when someone stalled, he looked for the real blockage underneath. Years of mentoring people through twelve-step recovery sharpened his eye for blind spots, unconscious decisions, and self-sabotage.

Yermi calls himself a bridger — a term from Dr. Lynn Weiss's Embracing A.D.D. He shares the ADHD wiring of the people he works with, and he's learned to translate it into the linear, structured world they have to operate in. He doesn't coach ADHD from a textbook. He coaches it from the inside.

Today he works with ADHD professionals full-time, grounding his practice in neuroscience and lived experience. He lives in Toronto with his wife and kids.

The Brilliant Brain (working title)

Chapter One · The Lie of the Lazy Genius

For most of your life you've been handed the same quiet verdict: you're so smart — if only you'd apply yourself. You nodded along. You believed it. And every missed deadline, every unanswered email, every brilliant idea that never made it out of your head became more evidence in a case you were building against yourself.

But what if the verdict is wrong? What if you have been applying yourself — ferociously, exhaustingly — just to keep pace with a world that was never designed for the way you think? The problem was never effort. The problem was the manual. You were handed someone else's, and told to make it work.

This book is a different manual. It starts with a single, radical premise: there is nothing about your mind that needs to be fixed. There is only a brilliance that hasn't yet been given the structure to shine.
